Long Island: Seaside views, cozy cafés, and vintage towns—every moment feels like a vacation film.

Long Island offers an ideal blend of coastal scenery, cultural attractions, and relaxing getaway spots just outside New York City. From pristine beaches and historic lighthouses to charming villages and local wineries, it’s a destination suited for every type of traveler. Whether you’re seeking a peaceful retreat or a weekend full of activities, Long Island delivers a refreshing travel experience with plenty to explore.

The Sands Point Preserve

Often known as Hempstead House, this area may not be large, but it offers an impressive mix of a castle, a beach, and an elegant mansion—making it a diverse and worthwhile stop for visitors.

Old Westbury Gardens

Known as one of the most beautiful English-style gardens on Long Island, this estate was built in 1903 as a gift from a steel magnate to his fiancée. The grounds are expansive, though largely without shade, making it an ideal spot to visit on cloudy days—and a perfect place for a relaxing picnic.

Montauk Point Lighthouse

Montauk is the easternmost town on Long Island, and at the edge of the village you’ll find a picturesque lighthouse, ocean-facing swings, and a beach that offers a striking “end-of-the-world” feeling.

Fire Island

Fire Island is a narrow barrier beach just off the main part of Long Island, home to the tallest lighthouse in New York State—a popular spot for ocean views and photography.

Planting Fields Arboretum State Historic Park

Open to the public for free, the grounds feature expansive lawns and vibrant flowerbeds across 409 acres. It’s especially stunning in summer when the flowers are in full bloom.

Vanderbilt Museum

This is a popular Instagram-worthy spot on Long Island, featuring Spanish-style mansions, museums, and an observatory, along with a back garden overlooking the ocean dotted with sailboats.
